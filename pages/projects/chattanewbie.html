Consumer-Facing Website Collaboration

Chattanewbie

Project Overview

Chattanewbie was a project that I collaborated on over the course of several months through CodeXX, a women’s coding collective exclusive to Chattanooga. It was imagined to be a consumer-facing website/app that would connect Chattanooga locals with new residents through profiles and pre-generated, experience-driven “paths” that allowed users to uncover hidden places without the aid of a local.

The bulk of this work was accomplished at weekly meetups, and two hack-a-thons: one sponsored by the Lamp Post Group and another, multi-city hack-a-thon, Gig Hacks, which was sponsored locally by Mozilla.

Process

This project was very much a group effort and utilized the unique talents of everyone involved. We began by brainstorming and then mapping site interactions using personas that we crafted around who we perceived our users to be.

Once we had solidified the site structure, we took our ideas to production at weekend-long hack-a-thons where we would each work on one component of the site and then convene with the group and explain all that we had accomplished for the day. I was tasked with tweaking the original wireframe that another group member had provided and creating assets used throughout our working prototype.

At the end of each hack-a-thon, we presented all that we had accomplished during the weekend. We shared our work with team members via Google Docs and Git Hub. At Gig Hacks Chattanooga, our group was awarded for our pitch.
